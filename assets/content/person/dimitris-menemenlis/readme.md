Dimitris Menemenlis is a research scientist at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL). His research interests pertain to studies of the global ocean circulation and its interaction with land ice, sea ice, and biogeochemical cycles; the utilization and interpretation of satellite and in-situ ocean and sea ice data; and the development and application of parameterizations and data assimilation strategies for the Massachusetts Institute of Technology general circulation model (MITgcm). Among various projects, Menemenlis leads Estimating the Circulation and Climate of Ocean-Ice interactions in Earth System models (ECCO-IcES) and is a member of Carbon Monitoring System Flux estimation and attribution (CMS-Flux).
