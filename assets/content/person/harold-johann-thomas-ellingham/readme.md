Harold J. T. (H.J.T.) Ellingham (1897â€“1975) was a British physical chemist, best known for his Ellingham diagrams, which summarize a large amount of information concerning extractive metallurgy. He studied at the Royal College of Science, which was then a part of Imperial College London, from 1914 to 1916. He became a demonstrator at the college in 1919 and a reader in physical chemistry in 1937. Ellingham was acting secretary of the Royal College of Science from 1940 to 1944 and of the Royal Institute of Chemistry from 1944 to 1963. He was made a fellow of Imperial College in 1949 and an Officer of the Order of the British Empire (OOBE) in 1962. From 1964 to 1970, he worked as a special assistant at Imperial College.
